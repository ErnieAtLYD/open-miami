WE ARE ON A MISSION

To champion open-source technology in providing and enhancing municipal services and programs for community benefit.

To assemble and educate Miami-Dade County residents in engaging with the organizations making Miami a better place to live.

HOW?

Events & Community Building

Continuing and deepening the existing partnerships with Miami-Dade County, its 34 municipalities, non-profits and Foundations.

Investigate programs offering more focused and productive events related to a municipality or a topic, or multi-month technology fellowships or “challenges.”

Develop funding, training, and mentorship opportunities for civic entrepreneurs and people new to programming and coding such as Code School Graduates.

Technology training for nonprofits and municipal employees.

Organize events to bring people together across sectors.

Enhancing Services & Programs For Good

Do real science. Conduct data-backed experiments with the goal of demonstrating impact, provoking thought and inspiring civic-minded entrepreneurs. 

Iterate on our existing Monday “open hack night” collaboration space through fixed events and mailing lists.

Iterate on the CUTGroup Miami program to bring in residents and encourage further interactions.

Source, scope, and track ongoing project opportunities on the main civic issues from within the City and the nonprofit communities.

Organizational Infrastructure

Funding activities through a combination of classic non-profit models such as grants, donations, selling merchandise.

Build a cohesive brand to best communicate this parent organization and connect the various civic technology and open data efforts throughout Miami.

Recruit staff to manage the growing community.

Celebrate successful events, projects, and active members through storytelling.

Continue our existing “open book strategy” of being as open-source as possible.

Let's talk about us

Ernie Hsiung
Executive Director

Front-end developer and community organizer with interests in actual and virtual communities. From the Bay Area and living in Miami for the past four years, Ernie served as a 2015 Code For America fellow working with Miami-Dade County to create Civic Engagement and Economic Development technology. He is currently the Technical Director at WhereBy.Us, parent company to hyperlocal publications The New Tropic (Miami) and The Evergrey (Seattle).
